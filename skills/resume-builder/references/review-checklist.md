# Resume Review Checklist

Run this before handing off a resume.

## First Screen

- Is the target role obvious in under 3 seconds?
- Is the strongest differentiator visible without scrolling?
- Are `core skills` early enough for a recruiter scan?
- Does the summary fit in one quick read?

## Line Breaks

- Any metric value wrapping awkwardly?
- Any date range wrapping awkwardly?
- Any left-column label wrapping into a clumsy shape?
- Any header line mixing incompatible content?

## Layout Balance

- Is the top margin formal enough?
- Is page 1 bottom whitespace reasonable?
- Does page 2 begin at a comfortable vertical position?
- Are section gaps consistent?
- Is text density even, or does one area feel cramped next to a large empty area?

## Section Logic

- Are `core skills` before the reader needs them?
- Are certificates and job target separated?
- Is low-value content taking too much space?
- Is any section present only because the template had it?

## Print vs Screen

- Does PDF still look correct if screen-only styles are removed?
- Is the body background pure white for print?
- Are shadow and paper-frame effects limited to screen preview?

## Final Technical Checks

- Expected page count still correct?
- No unresolved placeholders?
- No invented metrics, dates, or ownership claims?
