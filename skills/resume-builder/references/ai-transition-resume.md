# Technical To AI Resume Strategy

Use this when the candidate is moving from technical, systems, testing, infrastructure, delivery, or adjacent engineering work into AI-focused roles.

## Core Story

Do not present the candidate as `starting from zero in AI`.

Present the story as:

- strong engineering base
- real workflow understanding
- recent AI application practice
- ability to connect models to production constraints

## What To Emphasize

- integration and systems thinking
- real-world tooling and workflow adoption
- evaluation, testing, debugging, and delivery discipline
- Python or adjacent implementation ability
- practical AI use in coding, review, retrieval, automation, or internal tools

## What To Downplay

- broad speculative job lists
- buzzword-heavy summaries
- AI enthusiasm without concrete shipped workflows
- long lists of tools without a use case

## Recommended Summary Shape

1. current engineering role and years of experience
2. recent AI practice window
3. concrete areas: Agent, MCP, RAG, AI Coding, evaluation, internal tools
4. why this matters: can connect model capability into existing systems and workflows

## Section Strategy

- Move `core skills` earlier than usual.
- Put the most AI-relevant projects before legacy experience detail.
- Keep legacy experience, but frame it as proof of engineering depth.
- Use project wording that shows `tooling -> workflow -> outcome`.

## Resume Risk Signals

Avoid these patterns:

- too much old-domain detail before any AI signal
- skills hidden on page 2
- generic AI section with no workflow examples
- summary that reads like a learning diary instead of a hiring pitch
